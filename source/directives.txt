Analytics Engineer Take-Home Assignment
Welcome to the technical assessment!
This assignment simulates a real-world engineering and analytics problem. It is designed to evaluate your T-SQL data modeling skills, your ability to leverage Python for advanced
data engineering in a modern lakehouse architecture along with your business data analysis acumen.
Scenario
You are working in our Microsoft Fabric environment. The upstream engineering teams have landed raw operational data into our lakehouse delta tables.
However, the data is unmodelled, messy, and unoptimized for reporting.
The executive leadership team needs answers to a critical business question:

Which customer industries are generating sustainable revenue versus those consuming an unsustainable volume of our engineering and support resources?

Your goal is to build a refined data pipeline and deliver a final analytical summary that bridges the gap between raw data and executive strategy.

Provided Datasets : You have access to three tables in the lakehouse (assume standard MS SQL / T-SQL syntax for SQL operations):
1. dbo.raw_companies: Internal CRM registry of enterprise clients.
    Fields: company_id (VARCHAR), company_name (VARCHAR), created_at (DATETIME), industry (VARCHAR)
2. dbo.raw_subscriptions: Subscription history logs.
    Fields: subscription_id (VARCHAR), company_id (VARCHAR), plan_name (VARCHAR), monthly_amount (DECIMAL), status (VARCHAR: 'active', 'cancelled'), start_date (DATE), end_date (DATE)
3. dbo.raw_support_tickets: Support system dumps.
    Fields: ticket_id (VARCHAR), company_id (VARCHAR), created_at (DATETIME), resolution_time_hours (DECIMAL), status (VARCHAR: 'solved', 'open'), telemetry_metadata (VARCHAR - Messy
    JSON string containing browser, OS, and priority info).

Assignment
Task 1: T-SQL Data Modeling (Fabric Data Warehouse)
    Write clean, production-grade T-SQL to transform these raw tables into a clean, dimensional star schema. Please implement a decoupled staging layer and a final integrated analytical
    wide table or fact table (e.g., fact_company_monthly_metrics). Final output should support analysis at Company × Month grain.
    Business Logic Constraints:
    1. Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR): Extract the revenue generated only for months where the subscription status was active. Ensure MRR is correctly represented across the lifespan of
    each subscription (including multi-month subscriptions).
    2. Support Load Score: Calculate a custom metric to quantify support weight. Please combine both ticket volume and engineering effort (e.g. resolution hours):

Task 2: Python Notebook Case Study (Fabric Lakehouse)
Switching roles to a notebook-based workflow, open a Python environment (pandas or PySpark) to handle the unstructured elements of our data and perform advanced cohort calculations:
    1.  Extract the nested priority (High, Medium, Low) from the messy telemetry_metadata JSON string column in dbo.raw_support_tickets.
    2.  Write a Python function to flag any company that experienced a month-over-month (MoM) spike in High-Priority tickets greater than 200%. Ensure your logic handles cases where
    the previous month has zero observations.
    3.  Merge this back into a clean data frame and outline how you would save it back as a Delta table in the Fabric Lakehouse.

Task 3: Data Analysis & Business Strategy
Do not just move the data, interpret it. Based on the data model you designed, write a short analytical memo answering the following questions for our executive team:
    1. If an industry has a rapidly growing MRR but its Support Load Score is increasing at twice that rate, what strategy should the account management team take?
    2. Looking at your final data layout, how would you define a "toxic" customer segment? What combination of data thresholds would you use to flag them automatically?
Your answer should include both a definition (metrics/thresholds) and a recommended action.

Task 4: Testing & Documentation
Provide a brief README.md file including:
- T-SQL or dbt-style verification queries ensuring primary keys are unique and financial fields contain no negative values.
- A final T-SQL query that an analyst can copy/paste into Fabric to find the Top 5 most expensive industries to support relative to their MRR contribution.

Submission Guidelines
1. You have 5 days to complete the assignment (Expected effort: 2-3 hours).
2. Deliverables: A Git repository link or zip file containing your .sql files, a Python notebook (.ipynb or raw script), and your README.md.
3. In addition, you can include a 3-minute video walkthrough sharing your screen, explaining your modeling choices and your Python case study logic.
This is optional, as all assignments will be assessed, and you will be invited to a short (30 mins) technical review where you will provide a live walkthrough sharing your screen.
The session will also include a live modification challenge, where you will be asked to alter your code on the spot.

Next Round
Successful submissions move to a Presentation/final interview.
We treat our Analytics Engineers as strategic partners, not just ticket-takers. Therefore, you should treat the first 10 minutes of this interview as a formal presentation
to our Executive Team (played by your interviewers). Present your findings from Task 3: Data Analysis & Business Strategy.
Explain which customer segments are "toxic," which industries are driving sustainable growth, and your strategic recommendations for management.
You may use 2-3 slides, or present cleanly from a visualisation/dashboard or your markdown file.
Your goal is to translate your data model's insights into clear, actionable business metrics for non-technical stakeholders.
